# Code of Conduct

FlixelGDX is a community-driven project, and we want it to stay that way. Whether you are a first-time contributor
opening your very first pull request or a long-time maintainer who has been here since the beginning, you are
welcome here, and we want to keep it that way.

This document explains the standards we hold everyone to, the behaviors we will not tolerate, and how we handle
situations where those standards are not met. These rules apply to all spaces connected to this project: GitHub
issues, pull request reviews, commit comments, discussion boards, and any spaces where people are clearly acting 
as representatives or discussing FlixelGDX.

---

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project a respectful, kind, and harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of age, background, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, level of experience,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We also pledge to extend that same respect to people who are new to open source. Learning in public is hard.
Nobody started out knowing how pull requests, merge conflicts, or Java generics worked. Be the person you
wished existed when you were just getting started.

---

## What We Expect

### Be respectful

Treat every person in this community with the same basic dignity you would want for yourself. Disagreements are
natural and healthy in a technical project; how you handle them is what matters.

- **Critique the code, not the person.** Saying "this approach has a performance problem because it allocates
  inside the update loop" is constructive. Saying "this is terrible code" is not.
- **Assume good intent first.** Most people asking a question or submitting a patch are genuinely trying to help
  or learn. Interpret ambiguous messages charitably before assuming the worst.
- **Use inclusive language.** Avoid slurs, derogatory terms, and language that excludes or demeans people. This
  applies to comments, commit messages, variable names, and documentation.
- **Respect time and effort.** If someone spent hours on a contribution that does not fit the project's direction,
  acknowledge the effort honestly before explaining why you are declining it.

### Be kind

Kindness is not weakness. It does not mean avoiding hard conversations or accepting bad code. It means having
those conversations in a way that provides clear, actionable, and objective feedback.

- **Welcome newcomers explicitly.** If you see someone's first issue or first PR, say so. A short "welcome to the
  project" makes a real difference to someone who is nervous about contributing to open source for the first time.
- **Offer help, not just rejection.** If a contribution is not ready to merge, explain what would make it ready.
  If you do not have time to explain, say so and point to the contributing guide rather than just closing without
  comment.
- **Celebrate good work.** When someone fixes a tough bug, writes clear documentation, or helps another contributor
  through a problem, say so. Positive feedback is as important as critical feedback.
- **Check in on people.** If a contributor goes quiet after receiving heavy review feedback, a short "Hey, are you
  still working on this? Let us know if you need help" goes a long way.

### Be collaborative

Open source works because people with different skills, backgrounds, and time zones build something together that
none of them could build alone. Collaboration requires trust, transparency, and communication.

- **Communicate early and often.** If you are working on a large feature, open a draft PR or discussion thread
  early so others can give feedback before you invest too much time going in the wrong direction.
- **Ask before assuming.** If a piece of code looks wrong or confusing, ask the author what they intended before
  rewriting it. There may be context you are missing.
- **Share your reasoning.** When you request changes on a PR, explain why. "Change this to use a pool" is less
  helpful than "change this to use a pool because this method is called every frame and the current approach
  allocates on every call."
- **Accept that you will be wrong sometimes.** Everyone makes mistakes, misses context, or learns something new.
  If someone points out a better approach, engage with it honestly rather than defending your original choice out
  of pride.
- **Give credit where it is due.** If you build on someone else's idea, say so. If you found the answer in a
  comment thread, link to it.

## What We Will Not Tolerate

The following behaviors are unacceptable in this community, regardless of who the person is or what their
intentions were.

- **Harassment and intimidation**: Threatening messages, deliberate intimidation, sustained disruption of
  discussions, or any behavior intended to make someone feel unsafe or unwelcome.
- **Personal attacks**: Insults, derogatory comments, or attacks on someone's identity, background, or level
  of experience.
- **Discrimination**: Treating someone differently or dismissively because of their age, background, disability,
  ethnicity, gender identity or expression, nationality, race, religion, sexual orientation, or any other
  personal characteristic.
- **Trolling and bad-faith participation**: Deliberately misleading others, derailing discussions, or engaging
  with the community solely to cause frustration or conflict.
- **Unwanted contact**: Sending unsolicited messages to community members outside of project-related spaces to
  pressure, harass, or recruit them.
- **Publishing private information**: Sharing someone's personal information (real name, email address, location,
  and so on) without their explicit consent.
- **Retaliation**: Taking negative action against someone for reporting a conduct issue or for supporting someone
  who did.
- **Predatory behavior**: Engaging in behavior that is intended to exploit someone for personal gain, such as
  sexual harassment or grooming.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including but not limited to:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and comments
- The project's official communication channels (Discord, forums, and similar)
- Any event or meeting organized by the project
- Any public space where someone is clearly acting as a representative of FlixelGDX

---

## Reporting

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it. You can reach the
head maintainer by email at **`stringfromjava@gmail.com`** or on Discord with the handle **`publicstaticstring`**. 
Reports are treated with discretion and confidentiality.

When you report an incident, try to include:

- Your contact information (if you are comfortable sharing it)
- A description of what happened, including any relevant links or screenshots
- The names or usernames of the people involved
- Any context you think is relevant

You do not need to have a complete picture to file a report. If something felt wrong, say so and let us
investigate.

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct. When a report is received, they
will review the situation and determine an appropriate response, which may include any of the following:

1. **A private reminder**: For minor or first-time violations, a private message clarifying which behavior
   violated the Code of Conduct and what is expected going forward.
2. **A public warning**: For more serious or repeated violations, a public note on the relevant thread or
   discussion explaining that a behavior is not acceptable.
3. **Temporary suspension**: Removal from project spaces for a defined period of time. During this period the
   person is expected not to interact with the project or its community.
4. **Permanent ban**: For severe violations or a sustained pattern of behavior that harms the community, permanent
   removal from all project spaces.

Maintainers will make enforcement decisions based on the specifics of each situation. They reserve the right to
remove, edit, or reject any contribution, comment, or other content that does not meet these standards, and to
ban any contributor whose behavior is harmful to the community.
